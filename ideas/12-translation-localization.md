# Idea 12 — AI Translation & Localization Agency

**Category:** Small Business Services / Entrepreneurship & Job Creation
**One-liner:** Translate your website, docs, and product copy into 30 languages overnight — culturally tuned, not Google-translated.

## The business
- Target: SMBs entering new markets, e-commerce sellers (Shopify), SaaS expanding to LatAm/EU/Asia.
- Pricing: $0.04/word (vs. $0.15–0.25 from agencies), $99/mo for continuous website sync, $999 for full app + marketing localization.
- Revenue path: 50 customers × $250 avg = $12.5k.

## What the AI agents do
- Crawl client website, extract translatable content with context.
- Translate with cultural adaptation (idioms, currency, date formats, taboos).
- Run quality assurance: back-translate, check tone consistency, glossary compliance.
- Maintain translation memory per client (consistency across docs over time).
- Auto-sync: when source content changes, re-translate diff only.
- Optional human-reviewer-in-the-loop tier for marketing-critical copy.

## Why it can be AI-run
- LLM translation has caught up to professional translators on most language pairs.
- The "humans add value" parts (cultural nuance, brand voice) — agents can now handle with style guides.

## 90-day go-to-market
- Wk 1–2: Build for Shopify (one-click app: instant Spanish/French/German store).
- Wk 3–6: Shopify App Store launch + cold outreach to Shopify Plus merchants.
- Wk 7–12: Add SaaS angle — Stripe Press / Lemon Squeezy partnerships for docs translation.

## Tech stack
- Gemini long-context for whole-page consistency.
- Vertex AI Translation as fallback baseline.
- Cloud Firestore for translation memory.
- Per-language style guide retrieval pipeline.

## Risks & moats
- **Risk:** Google Translate is "good enough" for cheapskates. Need to clearly demonstrate quality delta.
- **Moat:** Translation memory + brand glossary per customer compounds with every project.
