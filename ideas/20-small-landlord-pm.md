# Idea 20 — AI Property Manager for Small Landlords

**Category:** Small Business Services
**One-liner:** Replace your 10% property manager. Your tenants don't even know it's AI.

## The business
- Target: "Mom-and-pop" landlords with 1–10 units. They currently DIY (and hate it) or pay 8–12% to a PM company.
- Pricing: $49/unit/mo (vs. ~$150/unit/mo from traditional PM for a $1500/mo unit).
- Revenue path: 50 landlords × 3 units × $49 = $7.5k MRR initial, fast scaling.

## What the AI agents do
- Tenant communication: Gemini Live answers calls/texts 24/7 (lease questions, payment, scheduling).
- Rent collection: auto-reminders, payment portal (Stripe), late fee enforcement per lease terms.
- Maintenance: triage tenant requests, dispatch from vetted vendor list, follow up to closure.
- Lease renewals + rent comp analysis when renewing.
- Vacancy management: write listing copy, post to Zillow + Apartments.com, screen applicants (credit + eviction history check via TransUnion SmartMove).
- Generate state-compliant notices (late rent, lease violation, eviction filing prep).

## Why it can be AI-run
- 80% of PM work is communication + scheduling + paperwork. All automatable.
- The 20% needing humans (in-person inspections, complex evictions) → contracted local handyman/attorney network.

## 90-day go-to-market
- Wk 1–2: Build for one state (Texas — landlord-friendly, large market).
- Wk 3–6: BiggerPockets forum, landlord Facebook groups, Roofstock investor outreach.
- Wk 7–12: Expand to FL, AZ, NC. Add insurance + tax referral revenue stream.

## Tech stack
- Gemini Live for inbound tenant calls.
- Twilio for SMS/voice.
- Stripe for rent collection.
- Zillow Rental Manager + Apartments.com APIs for listings.
- TransUnion SmartMove for screening.

## Risks & moats
- **Risk:** Real-estate-specific edge cases (security deposit law varies wildly by state). Start narrow.
- **Moat:** Each landlord's tenant history + lease docs = strong switching cost. Recurring revenue from day 1.
