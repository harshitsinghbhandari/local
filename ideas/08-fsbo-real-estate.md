# Idea 08 — AI Agent for FSBO Home Sellers

**Category:** Professional Services Access
**One-liner:** Sell your home without paying 6% — the AI does what the realtor would, and you keep $30k+.

## The business
- Target: For-Sale-By-Owner (FSBO) sellers in US. Selling a $500k home = $30k commission saved.
- Pricing: $999 flat fee per home sold, $1,999 full-service tier (paperwork + closing coordination).
- Revenue path: 20 closings × $1,500 avg in 90 days = $30k. Each closing takes 30–60 days, so launch fast.

## What the AI agents do
- CMA (comparable market analysis) using Zillow + Redfin + county records.
- Listing copy + professional photo direction (or coordinate a $200 photographer).
- MLS submission via flat-fee MLS partner.
- Buyer inquiry handling (text + email + voice) — qualify pre-approval, schedule showings.
- Negotiation support: counter-offer drafting, comp justification.
- Document checklist + e-sign coordination, escrow handoff.

## Why it can be AI-run
- Realtors charge 2.5–3% per side. 80% of that is admin work + buyer hand-holding.
- Negotiation is information asymmetry — the AI removes that gap.

## Regulatory note
- Not a licensed broker = stay in "marketing/admin" lane; partner with flat-fee MLS service for the listing piece.
- Vary by state — start in TX, FL where FSBO is least regulated.

## 90-day go-to-market
- Wk 1–2: Partner with one flat-fee MLS service. Build CMA + listing flow.
- Wk 3–6: Facebook ads to FSBO searchers. SEO for "sell home without realtor".
- Wk 7–12: Onboard transactions, document the first 5 closings as case studies.

## Tech stack
- Gemini for listing copy, negotiation drafting, document analysis.
- Vertex AI Search over MLS, Zillow, county data.
- Twilio for buyer call handling.
- DocuSign for closing docs.

## Risks & moats
- **Risk:** Closing requires 30–60 days — revenue recognition will lag past Aug 17. Start escrows now.
- **Moat:** Each closing = strong testimonial. FSBO market is referral-heavy.
