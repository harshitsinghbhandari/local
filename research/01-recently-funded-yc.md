## Notable Standouts (Traction Signals)

- **Cardboard** (W26) — agentic video editor; highest-upvoted HN launch in W26.
- **RunAnywhere** (W26) — on-device AI, 10.2K GitHub stars.
- **Pocket** (W26) — AI note-taking device, **$27M ARR, 30K+ units shipped** (extraordinary for a YC-batch company).
- **Light Anchor** (P26) — AI-operated consumer brands, the literal "AI is the company" thesis.
- **Carma** (W26) — already powering Fortune 500 fleets.

## What's Notable That ISN'T Happening

- **Almost zero AI tutoring / education consumer plays in W26.** YC seems to have written off consumer education (or thinks it's saturated). Compare to a16z thesis which is more bullish.
- **Tax prep / consumer finance** — surprisingly thin. Big opportunity.
- **Property management / small landlord ops** — only Bravi (home services), nothing tenant-facing. Gap.
- **Immigration consumer** — Legalos (W26) is law-firm-facing, not consumer-facing. Gap remains.
- **Translation/localization** — no W26 companies attacking this; Panjaya is the only post-2024 mover I see in adjacent space.

## What This Means

The shape of recent YC funding tells us:
1. **AI agents replacing specific job functions is the modal bet.** Pick a job title, build the agent.
2. **Vertical depth > horizontal breadth.** "AI accountant for dental practices" beats "AI accountant."
3. **B2B services >>> consumer AI.** The money is in replacing the $50k–$200k labor line item, not selling $20/mo subs.
4. **AI-native is now the floor, not the ceiling.** "Has AI" wins nothing; "is run by AI end-to-end" wins.
